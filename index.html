what is OpenSource?
software for which the original source code is made
  • freely available
  • redistributed and modified.
what is GSoC?
a annual program, in which Google pays university students for working as student developers in the open-source organisation.
TimeLine(1/2)
  • Feb 20: Organisations Announced
  • March 16-31: Students Application Period
TimeLine(2/2)
  • May 4: Results Announced
  • June 1 to August 31: Coding Period.
In 2020, GSoC timeline was shifted by 1 month due to pandemic.
Why you shouldParticipate? (1/3)
  • You get to choose (unlike engineering)
  • Flexible work (not always but often)
  • International connections
Why you shouldParticipate? (2/3)
  • Opportunity of foreign trip (only with some organizations)
  • Learn about tools, applications, environment and workflow
  • Sneak peek into open source (if you are new),
    how they manage and maintain different aspects of the project
Why you shouldParticipate? (3/3)
  • History of the project/org (if are into that kind of stuff)
  • Great people (mentors and community members),
    stipend, resume build-up,
    bragging rights (if famous org)
Why you shouldn't?
choosing the right Organisation
    Variety of Oraganizations
  • Java, C, C++, Python, C# .....
  • ML, Robotics, Web Dev, SE ....
    Based on Time in hand
  • YOU DONT'T KNOW ANYTHING - Start 3-4 months early, learn a skill and then contribute
  • Else start contributing 2 months before selection process
    Based on Popularity of Organization
  • More popular the organization( like Django, Linux Foundation, Git ), the greater the competition
  • Just getting selected in GSoC is EASY
    Not interested in SE or Web Dev
  • ML Organizations like OpenCV, Tensorflow
  • Hardware & Robotics orgs like Arduino, Open Robotics
how to contact theOrganisation?
Get familiar with the organization, by visiting their repositories, websites, bug-trackers, etc.
Join the Mailing Lists, IRC Channel, Forum of the organization.
Now, after this, you can do two things to make yourself being noticed in the community.
  • Find Low Hanging Bugs. Solve them and get noticed.
  • First Introduce , and then solve issues
Never hesitate for asking help.
